engine oil filter unit : removal - refitting
INTRODUCTION
WARNING  
Before carrying out any operation on a vehicle fitted with the Stop&Start system, always follow the safety instructions(see, vehicle : precautions for the repair )
Location and specifications (tightening torques, parts always to be replaced, etc.)(see, engine oil circuit assembly : exploded view ).
CAUTION  
Always check the oil level using the engine oil dipstick.
Do not exceed the maximum level on the engine oil dipstick (as this could damage the engine).
Correct the engine oil level if necessary before delivering the vehicle to the customer.
removal
removal preparation operation
  • Position the vehicle on a two-post lift(see, vehicle : towing and lifting ).
  • Disconnect the battery(see, 12 V battery : removal - refitting ).
  • Remove the engine undertray.
  • Drain the cooling system(see, coolant circuit assembly : draining - refilling ).
    X62/M9T/PA0,PF6/870
  • Remove:
    • the coolant circuit switchover valve(see, coolant circuit switchover valve : removal - refitting ),
    • the thermoplunger unit(see, heating element support unit : removal - refitting ),
    • the starter(see, starter : removal - refitting ).
    •  
  • Disconnect the oil pressure sensor connector(see, engine oil circuit assembly : exploded view ).
  • Remove the oil filter(see, engine oil circuit assembly : exploded view ).
    NOTE  
    Note:
    Prepare for the outflow of coolant.
removal operation
  • Use the toolRemote operation pliers for hose clips. (Mot. 1448)to separate the clip of the coolant-oil heat exchanger outlet pipe on the oil filter unit(see, coolant circuit assembly : exploded view ).
  • Disconnect the coolant-oil heat exchanger outlet pipe from the oil filter unit(see, coolant circuit assembly : exploded view ).
  • Remove(see, engine oil circuit assembly : exploded view ):
    • the oil filter unit,
    • the oil filter unit seal.
refitting
refitting preparation operation
  • CAUTION  
    To ensure proper sealing, the gasket surfaces must be clean, dry and not greasy (avoid any finger marks).
  • Usesurface cleaner (see, vehicle : parts and consumables for the repair )to clean and degrease:
    • the cylinder block gasket face,
    • the oil filter unit seal housings and gasket faces.
If replacing the oil filter unit
  • Remove:
    • the oil filter unit coolant pipe bolts(1),
    • the oil filter unit coolant pipe.
  • Remove the seals(2)from the oil filter unit coolant pipe.
  • Usesurface cleaner (see, vehicle : parts and consumables for the repair )to clean and degrease the seal housings of the oil filter unit coolant pipe.
  • Remove the oil pressure sensor from the old oil filter unit(see, engine oil circuit assembly : exploded view ).
  • Part to be routinely replaced: coolant-engine oil heat exchanger coolant outlet union seal
    .
  • Refit:
    • new seals on the oil filter unit coolant pipe,
    • the oil pressure sensor on the new oil filter unit(see, engine oil circuit assembly : exploded view ),
    • the oil filter unit coolant pipe.
  • Torque tighten thetighten to torque bolts of the oil filter unit coolant pipe at 12 N.mon the oil filter unit.
refitting operation
  • Proceed in the reverse order to removal.
  • Top up the engine oil level(see, engine oil : draining - refilling ).
    CAUTION  
    Always check the oil level using the engine oil dipstick.
    Do not exceed the maximum level on the dipstick (could destroy the engine).
    Correct the engine oil level if necessary before delivering the vehicle to the customer.
  • Before refitting the engine undertray, fill and bleed the engine cooling system(see, coolant circuit assembly : draining - refilling ).
